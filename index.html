Ancient History

Ancient history covers the earliest civilizations such as Mesopotamia, Egypt, Greece, and Rome. These societies laid the foundations for modern governance, philosophy, and science.

  • Mesopotamia - Cradle of Civilization
  • Ancient Egypt - Pyramids and Pharaohs
  • Ancient Greece - Democracy and Philosophy
  • Roman Empire - Law and Engineering

Medieval History

The medieval period, also known as the Middle Ages, lasted from the 5th to the 15th century. It was marked by feudalism, the rise of kingdoms, and the influence of religion.

  • Feudal System
  • The Crusades
  • The Black Death
  • Growth of Trade and Cities

Modern History

Modern history begins around the 16th century and includes major global transformations such as the Renaissance, Industrial Revolution, and World Wars.

  • Renaissance - Cultural Rebirth
  • Industrial Revolution - Technological Growth
  • World War I & II
  • Globalization and Digital Age
